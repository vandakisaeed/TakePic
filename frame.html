History

Nature photography has its roots in the early days of the camera, when explorers and scientists began using photography in the 19th century to document landscapes, wildlife, and plants. As cameras became more portable, artists and travelers alike started capturing the beauty of the natural world. Famous photographers like Ansel Adams helped elevate nature photography into an art form, showcasing the majesty of mountains, forests, and rivers. Today, with digital cameras and smartphones, people around the world can easily connect with nature through their lenses, continuing a long tradition of appreciation, exploration, and storytelling through images.
